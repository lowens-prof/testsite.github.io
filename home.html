Why Accessibility is Required

Making content accessible is a legal requirement as there are disability rights and legislation to ensure that people with disabilities aren’t disadvantaged in their daily lives. These provisions, including accessibility standards, fall under the American with Disabilities Act (ADA).

The Facts:

  • 1.3 billion people live with some form of low vision/blind (36 million of those people are blind). Here is a link to the 2019 statistics from World Health Organization.
  • 466 million people worldwide are hard of hearing. Here is a link to the 2019 statistics from the World Health Organization.
  • Nineteen percent of undergraduates in 2015–16 reported having a disability. Here is a link to the statistics from Nation center for Education Statistics.

Project Description

In today’s climate, people are more dependent on the internet more than ever. However, the internet is not always the most accessible area for people with disabilities. Examples below:
Disability Type Accessibility Barrier
Low Vision Web Content with bad contrast
Blind Images with no Alt Text (Screen Reader can’t read images)
Blind Formatting not compatible with Screen Reader (inaccessible charts and tables)
Our program will serve as an educational tool, where the user can experience what an inaccessible webpage looks like and how to convert the same intended content in an accessible format. There will be a button to toggle from the inaccessible webpage to the accessible webpage. The program will also explain how to implement techniques of creating accessible web content for the user and why it is important.

We will focus on the following areas for potential access barriers:

  • Contrast
  • Formatting
  • Alt Text Images
The outcome is to have the user gain the perspective of a person with a disability when observing or creating web content.
